Briefly describe the four gates explained in Chapter 11. Describe the behavior of each gate based on the input values to the gate.: AND Gates are made up of Two Inputs. Both Inputs need to be On (closed) in order for the Single Output to receive power. The OR Gate has Two Inputs, and One Output. But only One Input needs to be On (closed) in order for the Output to receive power. The Output also receives power if both Inputs are On (closed). NOR Gates are the exact opposite of OR Gates. NAND Gates are the exact opposite of AND Gates.
